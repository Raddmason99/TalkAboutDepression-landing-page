Listen to persons’ story as many times as they need to offer it and emphasize reasons for hope or future-oriented thinking.

Commit to spending time with the depressed person by engaging in productive and enjoyable activities. New thought processes that can replace suicidal ideation.

Suicidal people are often convinced they are inept losers. Your job is to prevent that self-image from perpetuation by reminding that person what they can do, have done, and can do again.

Ask the suicidal person for help on a project or to work out a problem.

Be a good example of step by step critical thinking. This means you are teaching your friend how to look for an alternative to a problem with a “can do attitude”.

Be a good example of self-reliance by encouraging self-efficacy, hope and a sense belonging become apparent in conversation.
